Metal Ministry Inc. is one of the prominent distributor and trader of SS 410 Bright Bars, which are generally considered to have slightly poorer weldability than the most common ferritic stainless steel grade. Special consideration of Stainless Steel 410 Square Bars should be given to avoid brittle weld fractures during fabrication by minimizing discontinuities, maintaining low weld heat input and occasionally warming the part somewhat before forming. A major difference can be attributed to the addition of Stainless Steel 410 Flat Bars to control hardening which results in the need for higher heat input to achieve penetration during arc welding. When weld filler is required, Stainless Steel 410 Threaded Bars is most often specified immersed in a warm solution of 10 % – 20 % nitric acid followed by a water rinse to remove any residual iron. Stainless Steel 410 Hollow Bars contains a minimum of 12 % chromium, just sufficient enough to give corrosion resistant properties. Stainless Steel 410 Hexagonal Bars has higher carbon content which is designed to optimize strength and hardness characteristics. Stainless Steel 410 Triangular Bars has good ductility it the annealed condition but is capable of being hardened the highest hardness of the 12 % chromium grades. Due to its hardening properties, Stainless Steel 410 Bars is not often welded, although it is possible. Its best corrosion resistance is achieved when Stainless Steel 410 Rods is hardened and surface ground or polished.
